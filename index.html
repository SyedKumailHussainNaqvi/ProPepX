ProPepX

A unified framework for interpretable bidirectional interaction-aware transfer learning in joint residue-level protein–peptide binding-site prediction

ProPepX learns molecular interfaces as reciprocal recognition events by allowing protein residues and peptide residues to exchange partner-conditioned information through bidirectional cross-interface attention.

Transfer-learning workflow

From generic binding data to task-specific interface prediction

The training design separates representation learning from task adaptation. Stage 1 learns general protein–peptide recognition principles from large-scale labeled binding data, while Stage 2 fine-tunes the pretrained ProPepX model for protein-side or peptide-side residue-level prediction.

  • Pretraining on diverse protein-binding and peptide-binding residue datasets.
  • Task-specific fine-tuning on smaller curated benchmarks.
  • Supervised transfer learning to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.

Architecture

Partner-aware model design

ProPepX integrates PLM embeddings, multi-scale motif extraction, transformer context encoding, bidirectional cross-attention, gated fusion, and residue-level classifiers.

01

PLM embeddings

Protein and peptide sequences are projected into contextual residue representations.

02

Intra-molecular encoding

Local motifs and long-range sequence dependencies are modeled before partner exchange.

03

Cross-interface attention

Protein→peptide and peptide→protein attention learns residue-residue coupling.

04

Gated fusion

Intrinsic self features and partner-aware cross features are adaptively integrated.

05

Residue classifier

Binding probabilities are generated for both molecular partners.

Interpretability

From prediction scores to mechanistic residue evidence

ProPepX provides bidirectional gated-weighted attention maps, attribution profiles, and perturbation analyses that help explain which partner residues support each residue-level binding-site decision.
