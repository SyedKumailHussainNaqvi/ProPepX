ProPepX interpretable bidirectional interaction-aware transfer learning

A unified framework for joint residue-level prediction of peptide-binding residues on proteins and protein-binding residues on peptides through reciprocal cross-interface reasoning.

Scientific rationale

Binding sites emerge from reciprocal protein–peptide recognition.

Protein–peptide interface residues cannot be treated as isolated sequence labels. A residue becomes functionally relevant through the molecular context of its interacting partner. ProPepX explicitly models this reciprocal communication with bidirectional cross-attention and gated fusion.

01

Unified prediction

Protein-side and peptide-side residue predictions are learned in one shared architecture.

02

Partner-conditioned features

Cross-attention transforms PLM embeddings into interaction-aware residue representations.

03

Data-efficient adaptation

Pretraining transfers general recognition knowledge to low-resource task-specific datasets.

Transfer-learning workflow

From generic binding data to task-specific interface prediction.

Stage 1 learns transferable protein–peptide recognition representations from large-scale labeled binding data. Stage 2 fine-tunes the pretrained model for protein-side or peptide-side residue-level prediction.

Architecture

Partner-aware model design.

ProPepX combines pretrained protein language model embeddings, multi-scale motif extraction, transformer contextual encoding, bidirectional cross-interface attention, gated fusion, and residue-level classification.

PLM embeddingsProtein and peptide sequences become contextual residue vectors.
Intra-molecular encodersLocal motifs and long-range context are learned before partner exchange.
Cross-interface attentionProtein residues attend to peptide motifs and peptide residues attend to protein interfaces.
Gated fusionSelf and partner-aware signals are adaptively integrated.
Residue classifierBinding probabilities are produced for both molecular partners.

Interpretability

From prediction scores to mechanistic residue evidence.

Bidirectional gated-weighted attention maps, attribution profiles, and perturbation curves connect residue-level predictions to interpretable protein–peptide coupling evidence.

  • Residue–residue cross-interface coupling maps
  • Top gated attention hotspots and recognition motifs
  • Attribution profiles for protein and peptide targets
  • Perturbation validation against random deletion controls

Biological relevance

Designed for interpretable peptide-recognition studies.

Low-resource transfer

Fine-tuning adapts pretrained interaction representations to small task-specific protein or peptide datasets.

Generalization

Zero-shot evaluation tests whether ProPepX captures transferable recognition rules rather than dataset-specific correlations.

Mechanistic inspection

Attention and attribution outputs support hotspot prioritization, interface analysis, and downstream peptide engineering.
