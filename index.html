Learning protein–peptide interfaces as reciprocal recognition events

ProPepX jointly predicts peptide-binding residues on proteins and protein-binding residues on peptides using bidirectional cross-interface attention, gated fusion, and supervised transfer learning.

Scientific rationale

Interface residues are not independent sequence labels.

A residue becomes functionally relevant through the molecular context of its interacting partner. ProPepX therefore conditions protein and peptide residue representations on each other, enabling partner-aware binding-site prediction.

01

Unified prediction

Protein-side and peptide-side residue predictions are learned within one shared architecture.

02

Partner-conditioned features

Cross-attention converts single-sequence embeddings into interaction-aware residue representations.

03

Transferable recognition

Large-scale pretraining transfers general molecular recognition knowledge to low-resource tasks.

Transfer-learning workflow

From generic binding data to task-specific interface prediction

Stage 1 learns broad protein–peptide recognition patterns. Stage 2 adapts the pretrained model to protein-side or peptide-side residue-level tasks.

Architecture

Partner-aware model design

ProPepX integrates PLM embeddings, multi-scale contextual encoding, bidirectional cross-attention, gated fusion, and residue-level classification.

01

PLM embeddings

Protein and peptide sequences are projected into contextual residue representations.

02

Intra-molecular encoding

Local motifs and long-range dependencies are captured before partner exchange.

03

Cross-interface attention

Protein↔peptide attention learns residue–residue coupling across the interface.

04

Gated fusion

Self features and partner-aware cross features are adaptively integrated.

05

Residue classifier

Binding probabilities are produced for each protein and peptide residue.

Why it matters

ProPepX turns interface prediction into interpretable molecular reasoning.

By explicitly modeling reciprocal communication between proteins and peptides, ProPepX supports data-efficient peptide engineering, interaction discovery, and therapeutic design workflows.
