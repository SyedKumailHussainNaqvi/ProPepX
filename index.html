Scientific rationale

Learning molecular interfaces as reciprocal recognition events

Protein–peptide binding sites are not independent sequence labels. A residue becomes functionally relevant through the context of its interacting partner. ProPepX therefore models both molecular partners together, allowing protein residues to attend to peptide motifs and peptide residues to attend to protein interface context.

Unified predictionProtein-side and peptide-side residue predictions are learned in one shared architecture.
Partner-conditioned featuresCross-attention turns single-sequence embeddings into interaction-aware residue representations.
Data-efficient adaptationLarge-scale pretraining transfers generalizable recognition knowledge to smaller task-specific datasets.

Training objective. Large-scale supervised interaction-aware pretraining followed by task-specific fine-tuning.

Transfer-learning workflow

From generic binding data to task-specific interface prediction

The proposed workflow separates representation learning from task adaptation: Stage 1 learns general protein–peptide recognition principles from large-scale labeled binding data, while Stage 2 fine-tunes the pre-trained ProPepX model for protein-side or peptide-side residue-level prediction.

Architecture

Partner-aware model design

ProPepX combines pretrained protein language model embeddings, multi-scale convolutional motif extraction, transformer contextual encoding, bidirectional cross-attention, gated fusion, and residue-level classification.

01

PLM embeddings

Protein and peptide sequences are projected into contextual residue representations.

02

Intra-molecular encoding

Local motifs and long-range dependencies are captured before inter-partner exchange.

03

Cross-interface attention

Protein↔peptide attention learns residue–residue coupling across the interface.

04

Gated fusion

Self features and partner-aware cross features are adaptively integrated.

05

Residue classifier

Binding probabilities are produced for each protein and peptide residue.

Interpretability

From prediction scores to mechanistic residue evidence

ProPepX provides bidirectional gated-weighted attention maps, attribution profiles, and perturbation curves that help explain which partner residues support each binding-site decision.

  • Residue–residue cross-interface coupling maps
  • Top gated attention hotspots and recognition motifs
  • Attribution profiles for protein and peptide targets
  • Perturbation validation against random deletion controls
Biological relevance

Designed for interpretable, data-efficient peptide-recognition studies

Low-resource benchmarks

Fine-tuning adapts pretrained interaction representations to small, task-specific protein or peptide datasets.

Generalization

Mechanistic inspection

Attention and attribution outputs support hotspot prioritization, interface analysis, and downstream peptide engineering.
